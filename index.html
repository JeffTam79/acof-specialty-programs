About ACOF

A Community of Friends (ACOF) is committed to ending homelessness for individuals living with mental health disabilities by providing permanent supportive housing. Guided by the Housing First approach, ACOF ensures that people experiencing chronic homelessness are offered immediate access to safe, stable housing without preconditions.

From there, we deliver integrated support services that promote long-term stability, wellness, and personal growth—creating a foundation for individuals and families to rebuild their lives with dignity and purpose.

Mission Statement

ACOF’s mission is to end homelessness through the provision of quality permanent supportive housing for people with mental illness.

The Housing First Approach

We follow a Housing First model—offering immediate access to safe, affordable housing with no preconditions—while delivering wraparound services that support stability, independence, and personal growth.

Who We Serve

Individuals with mental health and disabilities.

Families experiencing chronic homelessness.

Children affected by trauma and instability.

Children's Program

Provides essential support to children living in supportive housing. Through educational assistance, enrichment activities, and emotional well-being programs, we help children build confidence, succeed academically, and develop life skills for a brighter future.

Health & Housing

Stable and secure housing is the foundation for a thriving community. ACOF is committed to providing Permanent Supportive Housing (PSH) solutions for individuals experiencing chronic homelessness. Our housing programs offer long-term stability with integrated supportive services to ensure residents can maintain their homes and lead fulfilling lives.
